## Results

### Main conclusion from the provided output
Using the **synthetic prototype output only**, the proposed approach appears to satisfy the main targets:

| Objective | Pre-registered target | Reported synthetic result | Synthetic conclusion |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Training speed | ≥20% lower step time | 27.92% lower | Supported in simulation |
| Inference memory | ≥40% lower peak VRAM | 47.9% lower | Supported in simulation |
| Quality retention | <1% PPL degradation | +0.22% mean PPL | Plausibly supported, not definitive |

### Interpretation for the research question
For the question *“new research ideas to make NanoGPT faster, especially for small-batch training and low-memory inferen
ce”*, the synthetic output suggests:

1. **Small-batch training**
   - The biggest simulated gain likely comes from **shape stabilization + graph replay**, not just bucketing alone.
   - This supports the idea that NanoGPT inefficiency at small batch may be dominated by **launch overhead and under-occ
upancy**.

2. **Low-memory inference**
   - A simple low-bit KV baseline is already strong.
   - The adaptive policy is only truly interesting if it consistently beats **uniform INT4** on the **memory-quality fro
ntier**, not merely on memory alone.

3. **Research novelty implication**
   - If real results mirror the simulation, the publishable story is not “use `torch.compile` or quantization.”
   - It is more narrowly:
     - **training:** state-aware, shape-stable execution specialized for tiny batches;
     - **inference:** adaptive KV precision/retention that outperforms generic low-bit cache schemes.

### What is established vs speculative

| Claim type | Current status |
|---|---|
| The synthetic pipeline is internally coherent | Established from provided output |
| The hypothesis is feasible to test with the planned design | Supported |
| The intervention truly speeds up real NanoGPT | **Speculative** until real runs exist |
| The adaptive cache is publishably novel vs uniform INT4/KIVI/H2O-style baselines | **Speculative** until real ablation
s exist |

**Validation:** The reported synthetic results support the experimental logic and help prioritize ablations. Further act
ion is definitely needed: run real NanoGPT benchmarks before drawing scientific conclusions.
