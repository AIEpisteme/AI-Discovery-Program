\#\#\# 1) Key Findings\\ - **AI and scalable technologies dominate educational tech**: Exponential growth predicted in AI and computational thinking research through 2030, with concentrations in MOOCs; interdisciplinary links to healthcare emerging[1].\\ - **Textile tech shifts to smart materials**: Hotspots in nanotechnology, surface modification for antibacterial fabrics; emerging frontiers in smart textiles and silver nanoparticles since 2023, centered on materials science[2].\\ - **Skyrmionics as spintronics frontier**: Advances in magnetic skyrmions for spintronics/magnonics devices; progress in materials, imaging, dynamics, and architectures like antiskyrmions and hopfions[4].\\ - **Unconventional computing with nanotech**: Roadmap highlights beyond-Moore's Law approaches in nanotechnology for computing/signal processing, identifying dominant promising technologies[12].\\ - **AI for innovation knowledge discovery**: Exponential growth in ML/DL/NLP applications for patent analysis, technology trends, and scientometrics; led by China/USA collaborations (2010–2024)[8].\\ - **Frontier patterns in science/tech**: Emerging fronts identified via quantitative/qualitative methods show high-impact areas differ from high-growth ones in knowledge absorption[15].

\#\#\# 2) Methods Used in the Field\\ - **Bibliometric and forecasting tools**: CiteSpace, VOSviewer, Bibliometrix for mapping keywords, co-citations, institutions; ML forecasting for trends (e.g., 9,630 edtech articles 2020–2024)[1][2][7][8].\\ - **Network and scientometric analysis**: Co-occurrence, bibliometric coupling for hotspots (e.g., textile tech 2005–2025; AI in innovation 2010–2024)[2][3][8].\\ - **Experimental and quasi-experimental designs**: Posttest control groups testing models like ICARE+Desmos for math education (e.g., t-tests on conceptual understanding)[9].\\ - **Roadmap and modeling approaches**: Collective expert sections on theory, materials, dynamics (skyrmionics); holistic expert overviews for nanotech computing[4][12].\\ - **Database searches with PRISMA**: Scopus/Web of Science for trends (e.g., flipped classrooms, EVs in SCI up to 2026)[5][7].

\#\#\# 3) Contradictions or Open Questions\\ - **Growth plateaus and gaps**: Textile research output plateauing despite prior surge; edtech gaps in equity, open resources, micro-learning despite AI boom[1][2].\\ - **Impact vs. growth trade-offs**: High-impact fronts differ from high-growth ones in science/tech knowledge absorption patterns[15].\\ - **Integration challenges**: Need for AI-biology cooperation in textiles; stabilizing/controlling skyrmions for devices; ethical implications of edtech AI[1][2][4].\\ - **Vocational edtech limitations**: Rising flipped classroom publications (R²=0.1847) but insignificant vocational impact; underexplored benefits/challenges[7].\\ - **Governance for advanced AI**: Open questions on viability of international institutions (e.g., AI Safety Project, Frontier AI Collaborative) for global risks[13].
